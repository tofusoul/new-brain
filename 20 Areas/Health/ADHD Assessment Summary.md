## Background & History
### Developmental History
- Born in Taipei, Taiwan; emigrated to NZ at age 11
- Early childhood: "difficult child," hard to soothe, anxious, couldn't tolerate separation from mother
- Primary school in Taiwan: Teacher suggested "asylum" due to hyperactive behavior
- Significant difficulties with: homework completion, following instructions, losing things, distractibility
- Cultural factors: Symptoms explained as "disobedience" rather than neurobiological

### Educational History
- Variable academic performance despite high intellectual ability
- Primary school: Underachievement due to attentional problems
- Secondary school (Christchurch): Excelled in STEM, failed English (29% School Certificate)
- University: Philosophy degrees (BSc 2007, BA Hons 2008) with variable grades (A- to E)
- Possible dyslexia: Letter reversal, handwriting difficulties, reading struggles in English only

### Work History
- Multiple career changes: Engineering (dropped out), Computer Science, Psychology, Carpentry, Cooking, Publishing
- Currently: Assistant Project Manager in construction
- Pattern: Performs well when emotionally engaged, struggles with detail-heavy/boring tasks

## Current Functioning
### Strengths & Resources
- High intelligence and extensive vocabulary
- Strong insight and problem-solving strategies
- High motivation to change and achieve
- Supportive wife (Lil) who corroborates difficulties
- Developed compensatory strategies using technology

### Current Challenges
- **Inattention**: Difficulty sustaining attention, careless mistakes, disorganization
- **Executive Function**: Task completion, time management, following instructions
- **Memory**: Forgetfulness, losing things, absent-mindedness
- **Self-Concept**: Low self-esteem tied to ADHD-related failures
- **Daily Life**: Household organization impacts relationship with wife

### Co-occurring Issues
- **Depression**: Moderate symptoms (DASS-21), history of suicide attempts (ages 8, 16)
- **Anxiety**: Mild symptoms, specific worry "I'm not good enough" 50-60% of day
- **Stress**: Moderate symptoms (may reflect ADHD restlessness)
- **Grief**: Recent loss of baby approximately one year prior
- **Sleep**: Long-standing difficulties
- **Reading**: Significant struggles with English reading only (uses single-word app)

## Professional Recommendations
### 1. Medical Treatment
- Consult GP for consideration of slow-release stimulant therapy
- GP can consult psychiatrist (with this report) for Pharmac special authority

### 2. Psychological Therapy
- **Cognitive-Behavioural Therapy (CBT)**: Address maladaptive beliefs from untreated ADHD
- **Schema Therapy**: Improve self-concept and reduce early negative beliefs
- **EMDR Therapy**: Process trauma related to ADHD experiences

### 3. Additional Assessments
- **Speech and Language Therapy**: Assess and treat English reading barriers if ADHD treatment doesn't resolve them
- **Specific Learning Disability assessment**: Formal evaluation for possible dyslexia

### 4. Support & Education
- **National ADHD Organizations**: ADHD.org.nz and adhd.co.nz
- **Support Groups**: Connect with others who have ADHD
- **Family Education**: Help wife and family understand ADHD

### 5. Recommended Reading
- "Taking Charge of ADHD" by Russell Barkley
- "Driven to Distraction" and "Answers to Distraction" by Hallowell & Ratey
- "Out of the Fog" by Murphy & Levert
- "ADHD in Adults: What the Science Says" by Russell A. Barkley
- "Is it you, me, or Adult ADHD?" by Gina Pera
