### Finding the Right Path
A career path should have:
1. Work that helps others
2. Work you're good at
3. Engaging work (autonomy, clear tasks, feedback, variety)
4. Colleagues you get on with
5. Fair pay and reasonable hours
6. A job that fits with the rest of your life

### Specific Ideas

#### Travel Site
Letting people own their travels.
**Problem:** TripAdvisor lacks trust in reviews
**Solution:** Focus on person - better judgments about what they say

#### Give Away Traders
Website connecting people who want to give away stuff to those who could make good use of it

### Business Type: Lifestyle Business

**Characteristics:**
- Definitely a social enterprise
- Focus on passive income
- Maximize job satisfaction
- Low risk, moderate return
- Low capital, no investors
- Small market with low competition
- Linear or capped growth
- Can be done on your own

**Requirements:**
- Technical skills most important
- Enjoy process
- Broad skill set
- Interest in marketing
- Aim to work for both small and large companies

### Monetization Strategies
- Support model (Richard Stallman)
- Freemium
- Subscription
- Paid products
- Personal brand (consultancy gigs)
- Marketplace
- Advertising
- Donation

## Business Planning Framework

### Business Development
- Come up with a brief for each idea
- Infrastructure to build
- System for keeping track of accounts (beancount and fava)
- Setup system for recording and sharing key learning effortlessly
- Setup Nextcloud

### Business Plans
- Do this if it will incur costs
- What are competitions like?
- Immutable advantage
- How is market changing?
- Be first to market with minimum feature set

### Marketing Plan
Plan marketing before product:
- Research customers and their industry
- Find influential people or become influential
- Establish business relationships
- Loss leader/freemium
- Grandfathering (first users free)
- Free products to influential people
- Careful what you give away (taking back causes discontent)
- Think about advertising avenues

### Value Proposition
- What is the problem?
- What itch does it scratch?
- Prototype: Tangible representation of idea
- Minimum Viable Product: Someone should desire it
- Be prepared to accept feedback
- What people do with something is way more reliable than what they say

### OKR (Objectives and Key Results)
Set clear objectives, document measure, build story
