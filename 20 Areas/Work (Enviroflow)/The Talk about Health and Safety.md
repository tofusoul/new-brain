- 2. Getting a system in place

- Need a system because no-alone can make a work place safe for them selves.

- The company as a whole has to be setup to deal with the risks we are exposing ourselves to each day.

- The **company as a whole and not just each of us individually** needs to develop:

1.  better awareness about the **hazards** we face each day to get the job done

2.  **build better habits** on how to deal with them

- **Systems are just** ways where every part does something expected, and together they make something valuable happen.

- e.g. SS drains. every part like pipes traps, slope, vents etc. carries the shit away smoothly at a press of a button without anyone having to think and deal with it.

- The kind of system we have these days are what rocket scientists invented did after Apollo 1. It works

- the idea is simple - everyone note down the risk and hazards that are noticed.

- Everyone sits down and spends some time regular to think about ways to stop exposing people to the risks and hazards.

1.  try doing things those ways.

2.  make it better

- For a system to work, everyone has to do their part including people that make the big decisions and the pencil pushers.

- A system with people is not like an engine or like a drain, where you can look at it and see what\'s not working, and know what to fix. Without writing it down, we won\'t find out reliably why things happen when things go wrong.

- Things need to be written down because it keeps people accountable and honest

- Example is, if you spot a risk or a hazard, wrote it down, and nothing is done about it, you can keep the company honest about looking after its people for real.

- writing it down makes the right people aware

- There are just a few things we are asking:

- Taking some time to spot the hazards we are exposing ourselves to.

- thinking about what to do about the hazards we spot, including prioritising what changes we work on.

- Getting proper Training

- talking about what to do (includes writing it down)

- doing what we talked about (so we know if it works or not )

- if works after a proper try, making a habit of it.

- Even though there is a bit written down:

- Changing habits is long and slow. So we will only focus on a couple of things each month.

- This month we are focusing on getting a habit of doing the paper work

- The main we need to become more aware of the risks of the work, so the focus this month is incidents and near miss reporting.
