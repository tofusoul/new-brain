- Being Stuck

I can never read much of *A Tale of Two Cities* because I can\'t picuture the rest of the book being any better than what is expressed at it\'s very begining:

> It was the best of times, it was the worst of times, it was the age of wisdom, it was the age of foolishness, it was the epoch of belief, it was the epoch of incredulity, it was the season of Light, it was the season of Darkness, it was the spring of hope, it was the winter of despair, we had everything before us, we had nothing before us, ...

For me, the book doesn\'t seem to climb up from that point. When you get to the \'the king with the large jaw...\' I begin to be suspicious of the direction to story is heading, and doubtful the story will captivate me more than that. I remember why I stopped reading this heavy tome last time.

When I look at my life carefully, It always is the best of time, it always is the worst of time, ceaslessly. I can\'t relate as much to the of the story, and the words arn\'t nearly as beautiful.

I am stuck, on many levels, this is why I started this blog and invited Poncho along. Maybe a voice, once found will get me unstuck. Maybe if my thoughts flowed, smoothly in words out of me and into a page, that flow turns to a movement that will carry me forward. What ever forward means.

It is the worst of times, it is the best of times. Where the conversation I am about to have with you, starts. My story

3 years ago, my wife and I lost Elliot, our 2nd child. Not long before this, my nephew threw himself the 5th floor of the building where my mum\'s whole family lives and died. Not long before then, my grandma passed away. Not long after Elliot, my cousin lost her battle to lukemia.

It was the worst of times. For a sheltered first world dweller like me.

- Elliot
