Hi Stephan, I will comment on your CV in order that you wrote it. I am putting myself in the hiring manager\'s shoes, but I do not know recruitment very well, so take this with a grain of salt and excercise your own judgment. If it feels right, you should probably stick with it.

1.  The profile and personal summary does a similar job, you might want to consider making these one section with fewer words instead.

2.  If you are only applying for recruitment consultant jobs taylor this CV to it. By this I mean you should focus on the traits that a great recruitment consultant needs. What makes you a great recruiter, what drives you as a recruiter, how do you succeed in your job, etc.

3.  People don\'t spend a lot of time going through CVs, so it\'s got to be succinct (clear, short and punchy expressions). Don\'t say anything twice.

4.  e.g. I would say something like this \"I am a recruitment consultant recruiting in the residential and light commercial construction market. My background as an experienced builder has led me to reliably succeed as a recruiter in construciton. My understanding of the Industry, along with my deep motivativation to grow my career as a recruiter has led to consistantly satisfied clients and candidates. I am seeking opportunity to futher my passiona and success in Auckland. I am adaptable, hard working, results focused and approach all things with a \'can do\' attitude. I am commited to this career and appriciate all challenges and opportunities that comes with construciton recuritment.\" which makes your situation clear and gives an indication of how you would work, and covers both sections.

5.  Skills section: you can make it less wordy by sumarising the activities you are confident in doing, make it bullet points. Don\'t focus too much on your character I think, your experience and your goals should speak for itself, and the interview is where people find that out anyway. What I mean is something like this:

    -   Construction Tallent Acqusition
    -   Candidate Screening
    -   Temp labour management
    -   Account managment
    -   Sales
    -   Cold call
    -   Capturing Client Brief.
    -   Interview
    -   Leadership I don\'t believe people pay too much mind to positive attributes stated in CVs. They just want to know that your past and your skills makes you suitable for the job your CV should show your understanding of the job. Specific skills are better than soft skills, becaueeveryone claims to have those soft skills.

6.  Experience section

    -   don\'t need to write about the duration in the post, that is explicit in the datesin sought after jobs, a lot of CVs are reviewed.
    -   I\'m not sure if the recuiter will understand things like \"cold desk\" only use terms like this if you are sure that it\'s something in common use.
    -   remove \"what I have learnt and delivered to date in my role\" that\'s redundant, you are telling the reader something that weakens your achievements and the responsibilities you took on, and it\'s extra wordsthat they won\'t have the patience to read.
    -   something like \"Sourced 12 new clients and retained their patronage\" speaks for itself, and is all they want to know
    -   focus on your responsibilities, and what you delivered, it will show what you can do. What you learnt should be obvious to the hirer. so don\'t say things like \"strengthened my sales skills and business acumen\" the fact that you made 12 new client for the company and delivered what they needed speaks for that.
    -   so instead of saying \"learnt how to conduct interviews\" you say \"responsible for interviewing all candidates in the residential and light commercial division of trade assist.\" so they know what you know how to do.
    -   you are way underselling your achievements as a site manager for trade assist. again ommit what you\'ve learnt, talk about what you\'ve done. Saying \"improved ability to communicate evefectively at any level\" suggests that you had on confidence with your communication skills on the job and that you are \"better now\". Instead say something like \"Sole point of contact for client leading a crew of 40 at Canterbury University NEB building\" or something that shows you can talk to people at any level and create good outcomes. Paint them a picture of what you did inspite of challenges. If you have the numbers, use them, achieving what margin. Company landed a further contract with the client after your work.. Anything of that sort. indicate what impact you had on the projects etc. by saying what you did.
    -   ditto with the rest of your history. tell the story with what you were responsible for and how you got good things to happen.

    1.  References: They generally want a manager as a reference, and generally discount family friends. Have a chat with your manager when you are ready to apply. They are generally good about it. You could also ask a client. Basically someone who has a good view of how you are to work with. Someone who can verify what you claim.
