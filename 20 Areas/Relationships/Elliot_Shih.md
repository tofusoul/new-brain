- [ ] haven\'t really expressed my feelings about elliot poperly 

our middle child. who died before he was born

Baby Elliott \"Toby\" Shih was born on Wednesday 5th of October 2016 but died in-utero a day or two before then. We discovered that his heart was not beating while Lil\'s contractions were around 5 minutes apart. He was on this earth for almost exactly 9 months, he has never taken a breath, and he has not experienced much other than the warm embrace of his beautiful mama\'s womb. He was peaceful, pure and beautiful like an angel when we bid him farewells on the 13th of October in the chapel of the crematorium. We are going to plant a tree that blossoms in spring time to remember him by. He had died of what appears to be an umbilico chord accident, he was growing well and kicking strongly before then.

With his fleeting life, Elliot has shown me much about love. Many people have said that love is deep, immediate, and unconditional, but I\'ve never felt it so simply as when I held Elliot in my arms. Love *is* immediate, because we have had no time with Elliot, yet it is there in full. Love /is /unconditional, because there would be no exchange of words, gestures and things between us and Elliot, yet it is there in full. Love *is* deep, because despite the fact that his body is just ashes now he is certainly in the depth of our hearts forever.

With his fleeting life, Elliot has shown me much about loss. Seems that loss is less about what we had that we no longer have-- that just happens with the passage of time. But more about what could have been, if only only it isn\'t the end yet. What we have mourned for was all the joy that we could have had together with Elliot, and all that we would learn from each other as a family. The mourning was for the loss of a beautiful possibility together rather than something we already had.

- Attempt at a kids story for Dill and Luca

attempt-at-a-kids-story-for-dill-and-luca

Everyone thinks that it all starts when you are born,

and it ends when you die.

That kinda makes sense...

If you think that you are you, and I am me, and we,

are just you + me.

two people apart

you like apples, I like kiwifruit

she like durians, he hates cheese

each of us live just under our skin,

somewhere in the brain

dreaming and walking our own dreams

A funny copy of our mum and dads, shaped by the books we read,

the hills we climb,

the food we eat

the school we go to

and the house we live in

Elliot, your brother, died a day or two before he was born

We don\'t know if he likes kiwifruit or apples, durians or cheese

He has never breathed the air

never drank milk

We cried and smiled with him

We held him tight that afternoon

His chubby little frozen face sparkled in the afternoon sun

A few days later when we said good bye

Like you, he is a funny copy of your mum and dad,

But he has not cried nor giggled

he has not danced or said a word

He died before he was born.

We love him very much,

We know now that it doesn\'t start when you are born

We know now that it doesn\'t end when you are dead

Because We, are not just you and me, sitting next to each other, but something more than that.
