# Health - Diet

**Status:** Ongoing Responsibility
**Focus:** Well-planned vegetarian nutrition

## Core Principles

### The Vegetarian Advantage
A transition to a vegetarian diet offers significant health benefits when done intentionally. Prospective studies and clinical trials associate well-planned vegetarian diets with reduced risk of:
- Cardiovascular disease
- Type 2 diabetes
- Hypertension
- Certain types of cancer

### Critical Distinction
The health advantage comes from careful, intentional construction of a diet rich in nutrient-dense, whole plant foods, not just from eliminating meat.

**Key Principle:** A healthy vegetarian diet is defined not by what it excludes, but by what it includes.

### The Whole Foods Focus
- Diverse array of whole fruits, vegetables, legumes, nuts, seeds, and whole grains
- Avoid refined grains, sugar-sweetened beverages, and processed "faux meats" laden with sodium, unhealthy fats, and added sugars
- Protective effects derived from high intake of dietary fiber, vitamins, minerals, and beneficial phytonutrients

## Key Nutrients to Monitor

### Protein
**Myth busting:** Modern nutritional science shows rigid protein combining at each meal is unnecessary. The body maintains an amino acid reserve pool, allowing combination from different foods throughout the day.

**Complete Protein Sources:**
- Soy (tofu, tempeh, edamame)
- Quinoa
- Buckwheat
- Hemp and chia seeds
- Nutritional yeast

**Excellent Sources:**
- Lentils (18g per cup)
- Chickpeas (16g per cup)
- Tofu (22g per cup)
- Quinoa (8g per cup)

### Iron
Two forms: heme (animal, well-absorbed) and non-heme (plant, poorly absorbed).

**Vegetarian requirement:** Almost double recommended intake of iron.

**Excellent Sources:**
- Legumes (lentils, soybeans, tofu, tempeh, beans)
- Fortified breakfast cereals
- Whole grains (quinoa, oats, fortified breads)
- Dark leafy greens (spinach, kale)
- Dried fruits (apricots, raisins)

**Maximizing Absorption:**
- Pair iron-rich foods with vitamin C (tomatoes, bell peppers, citrus, broccoli)
- Avoid tea/coffee within 1 hour of iron-rich meals (tannins inhibit absorption)

### Vitamin B12
**Critical:** B12 is not naturally found in plant foods. Deficiency causes severe and irreversible anemia and neurological damage.

**Reliable Sources:**
- Dairy products and eggs (for lacto-ovo)
- **Fortified foods** (most dependable strategy)
  - Nutritional yeast (¼ cup provides well over 100% DV)
  - Fortified plant milks (soy, almond, oat)
  - Fortified breakfast cereals
  - Fortified meat analogues

**Supplementation:** Regular B12 supplement recommended, especially for vegans.

### Calcium & Vitamin D

**Excellent Plant-Based Calcium Sources:**
- Fortified plant milks/juices
- Calcium-set tofu
- Low-oxalate dark leafy greens (kale, collard, turnip, bok choy)
  - Note: Avoid high-oxalate sources (spinach, Swiss chard) as calcium
- Legumes, nuts, seeds (soybeans, white beans, almonds, tahini)

**Vitamin D Sources:**
- Fortified dairy/plant milks
- Fortified cereals
- Egg yolks
- Sun exposure (especially important in winter)

### Omega-3 Fatty Acids

**Types:**
- ALA (alpha-linolenic acid) - plant-based
- EPA & DHA - marine-based (body converts ALA inefficiently, <15%)

**Excellent ALA Sources:**
- Ground flaxseeds (richest plant source)
- Chia seeds
- Walnuts
- Hemp seeds
- Canola, soybean, flaxseed oils

**EPA/DHA Sources:**
- Seaweed (nori)
- Microalgae (spirulina)
- Algal oil supplements (most concentrated)

### Zinc & Iodine

**Zinc Sources (lower bioavailability from plants):**
- Legumes (chickpeas, lentils, beans)
- Tofu
- Whole grains
- Nuts (cashews, almonds)
- Seeds (pumpkin, sesame)
- Dairy and eggs

**Iodine:**
- **Iodized salt** (simplest and most reliable)
- Seaweed
- Dairy products

## Nutrient Summary Table

| Nutrient | Why Important | Main Challenge | Top Sources |
|----------|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| Protein | Muscle, bone, organ health | Ensuring variety for all amino acids | Legumes, soy, quinoa, nuts, seeds, eggs, dairy |
| Iron | Oxygen transport | Low bioavailability of non-heme iron | Legumes, fortified cereals, tofu, greens, quinoa. Pair with Vitamin C |
| Vitamin B12 | Red blood cells, nerve function | Not in plant foods naturally | Dairy, eggs, fortified foods, supplements |
| Calcium | Bone and teeth strength | Oxalates inhibit absorption in some plants | Dairy, fortified milks, calcium-set tofu, kale, tahini |
| Vitamin D | Calcium absorption, bone health | Limited dietary sources | Fortified milks/cereals, eggs, sun exposure |
| Omega-3s | Brain health, anti-inflammatory | Inefficient ALA to EPA/DHA conversion | Flax, chia, walnuts (ALA); Algae, algal oil (EPA/DHA) |
| Zinc | Immune function | Lower bioavailability from plants | Legumes, tofu, nuts, seeds, grains, dairy |
| Iodine | Thyroid function | Plant foods generally low | Iodized salt, dairy, seaweed |

## Meal Planning

### 7-Day Meal Plan Examples

**Day 1:**
- Breakfast: Oatmeal with berries & walnuts (ALA omega-3s)
- Lunch: Hearty lentil & vegetable soup with whole-grain bread (iron + vitamin C from tomatoes)
- Dinner: Black bean & sweet potato tostadas with avocado and cheese
- Snack: Apple with peanut butter

**Day 2:**
- Breakfast: Greek yogurt parfait with granola, peaches, chia seeds (protein, calcium)
- Lunch: Leftover lentil soup
- Dinner: Tofu & broccoli stir-fry with brown rice (calcium-set tofu)
- Snack: Handful of almonds

**Day 3:**
- Breakfast: Tofu scramble with onions, peppers, spinach, turmeric, nutritional yeast (B12)
- Lunch: Quinoa salad with chickpeas, cucumber, tomatoes, lemon-tahini dressing
- Dinner: Mushroom-quinoa veggie burgers with whole-grain bun and asparagus
- Snack: Cottage cheese with pineapple

## Kitchen Setup

### Foundational Pantry (Long-term Staples)

**Legumes (Dried & Canned):**
- Lentils (brown, green, red)
- Chickpeas
- Black beans
- Kidney beans
- White beans (cannellini, navy)

**Whole Grains:**
- Quinoa
- Brown rice
- Rolled oats
- Whole-wheat pasta
- Whole-grain bread
- Whole-wheat tortillas
- Barley
- Buckwheat

**Nuts, Seeds, & Butters:**
- Walnuts, almonds (raw, unsalted)
- Natural peanut butter
- Almond butter
- Chia seeds
- Ground flaxseed (refrigerate)
- Hemp seeds
- Pumpkin seeds
- Sesame seeds
- Tahini

**Oils, Vinegars, & Condiments:**
- Extra virgin olive oil
- Canola or avocado oil
- Sesame oil
- Apple cider vinegar
- Balsamic vinegar
- Low-sodium soy sauce or tamari
- Dijon mustard
- Hummus

**Flavor Enhancers:**
- Yellow onions, garlic
- Low-sodium vegetable broth
- Canned diced/crushed tomatoes
- Tomato paste
- Dried herbs & spices (oregano, basil, cumin, chili powder, curry powder, turmeric, smoked paprika, cinnamon)
- Iodized salt
- Black peppercorns

**Fortified Items:**
- Nutritional yeast (fortified with B12)
- Low-sugar breakfast cereal (fortified with B12 and iron)

### Weekly Fresh List

**Produce (Rainbow of Nutrients):**
- Dark leafy greens (kale, collard, spinach, swiss chard, mixed salad greens)
- Vitamin C powerhouses (bell peppers, broccoli, tomatoes, citrus, berries)
- Cruciferous vegetables (cauliflower, brussels sprouts)
- Root vegetables (carrots, sweet potatoes, beets)
- Other core vegetables (celery, mushrooms, zucchini, cucumber, asparagus)
- Fruits (apples, bananas, avocados, peaches, pears)
- Fresh herbs (cilantro, parsley, basil)

**Plant-Based Proteins:**
- Extra-firm tofu (calcium-set)
- Tempeh
- Edamame (frozen, shelled)

**Dairy and Eggs:**
- Eggs
- Plain Greek yogurt (unsweetened)
- Low-fat milk
- Low-fat cottage cheese
- Hard cheeses (parmesan, cheddar)

**Fortified Alternatives:**
- Unsweetened almond or soy milk (fortified with calcium, vitamin D, B12)
