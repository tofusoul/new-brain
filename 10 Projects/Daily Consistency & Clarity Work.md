## Patterns Identified (So Far)

### Note-taking & System Inconsistencies
- **Daily notes**: Extremely variable — some days 300+ words with detailed logs, others 2-3 sentences
- **Task sprawl**: 30+ strategies documented in ADHD Management but few implemented consistently
- **System hopping**: Todoist, Obsidian Copilot, various experiments — constant search for "the right tool"
- **Capture vs action**: Notes capture ideas well, but execution is the gap

### Task Management Patterns
- **Strategy documentation** ✓ vs **daily practice** ✗
- **Planning strength** ✓ vs **follow-through** ✗
- **Sidetracked markers**: Self-awareness is there, but distraction happens frequently
- **Guilt patterns**: "Another distraction" self-judgment when exploring systems

### Work-Life Integration Challenges
- **Context switching**: Work tasks, family chores, parenting duties all interleaved
- **Boundary blurring**: No clear separation between deep work and household responsibilities
- **Family stress**: Notes reflect underlying family tension ("not that happy", "anxiety without understanding")

### Emotional & Cognitive Patterns
- **Negative self-talk**: "I'm not good enough" (50-60% of day, from ADHD assessment)
- **Despair moments**: "Pits of despair" recorded in journals
- **Self-criticism**: Guilt around productivity and distractions
- **Overwhelm**: Too many goals, projects, habit changes simultaneously
- **Clear distinction established**: Daily notes vs Bean activities — separation achieved!
