# Free Press - Personal Publishing

**Status:** Idea/Reflection
**Type:** Creative Writing/Publishing
**Categories:** Publishing, Anarchism

## Inspiration

### Origin
Back in '98, I wanted to start a broadsheet. Got the idea when I got a typewriter in '97 from the city council's recycling shop. After greasing the joints, I couldn't stop typing.

### What I Created
- Poems
- Prose
- Music reviews
- Drawings with letters

### What I Did
- Folded the ones I was happy with
- Put them on counters at cafes around High Street
- Sat and had coffee while scribbling more

### The Experience
- Folks would pick them up and read them
- Sometimes someone would smile while reading
- I enjoyed those moments greatly
- Felt like I had found a voice

## Concept

### Philosophy
Personal, grassroots publishing outside traditional channels. Taking words directly to people in public spaces.

### Anarchist Element
- Bypassing traditional publishing gatekeepers
- Direct connection between writer and reader
- Using public spaces as distribution channels
- No profit motive, pure communication

## Modern Implementation

### How It Could Work Today
- Print broadsheets or zines
- Leave in cafes, libraries, bookstores
- Share digitally as PDFs
- Maybe QR codes to online content

### Goals
- Share creative work
- Connect with strangers
- Find moments of shared joy
- Practice writing regularly
