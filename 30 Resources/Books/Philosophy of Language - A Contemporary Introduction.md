# Philosophy of Language: A Contemporary Introduction

**Author:** William G. Lycan (William Rand Kenan Professor of Philosophy at University of North Carolina)

![[2026-02-23_file_123---94cdb5e6-7e14-47a9-81d4-9a21def6908a.jpg]]

## Overview

A contemporary introduction to philosophy of language for students. The book is structured into four main parts covering reference and referring, theories of meaning, pragmatics and speech acts, and theories of metaphor.

## Book Structure

### Part I: Reference and Referring
Covers Russell's Theory of Descriptions, Donnellan's distinction, and related topics.

### Part II: Theories of Meaning
Surveys competing theories of meaning in language.

### Part III: Pragmatics and Speech Acts
Covers indirect force, metaphor, and speech act theory.

### Part IV: Theories of Metaphor
Examines theories of metaphor (new to this edition).
