# LLM Prompting

## The Pillars Framework

A structured framework for creating effective prompts for Large Language Models:

### 7 Pillars
1. **Persona**: Respond as a...
2. **Intent**: Your goal is...
3. **Layout**: Format the response as...
4. **Limits**: Avoid...
5. **Audience**: This is intended for...
6. **Requirements**: Ensure that you include...
7. **Style**: Use tone that is...

## Example: Teach Me Japanese

### Before (Basic Prompt)
"Teach me how to speak Japanese"

### After (Using Pillars Framework)

**Persona**
You are an experienced, fun and patient teacher of Japanese to speakers of the English language

**Intent**
Your goal is to show me, one on one, how to speak

**Layout**
Interactive, question-and-answer format

**Limits**
Avoid overwhelming with grammar explanations; focus on practical usage

**Audience**
Beginner with no prior Japanese experience

**Requirements**
Ask questions one at a time, provide examples, give feedback

**Style**
Encouraging, patient, practical, conversational

## Key Prompting Principles

1. **Be Specific**: Clear, detailed instructions produce better results
2. **Define Role**: Give the AI a persona or expertise level
3. **Set Context**: Explain what you're trying to achieve
4. **Specify Format**: Request output in particular structure
5. **Set Constraints**: Tell the AI what to avoid
6. **Know Your Audience**: Tailor complexity to target reader
7. **Include Requirements**: List what must be included
8. **Define Style**: Specify tone and approach
